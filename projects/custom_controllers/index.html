Modded Controllers and Consoles

a collection of some of my different gaming controller and console mods.

I play games to mess with the controllers. Not the other way around. Don’t get it twisted.

My Fightstick

My personal fightstick.

As an avid fighting game player, it’s important I come with the style to local tournaments. My fightstick is a Qanba Pearl modded with Crown SDB-202 MX 30mm buttons clipped to fit into the Pearl and a IST Alpha 49S korean lever. Special shoutout to hugs_n_giggles for the artwork.

Another Fightstick

Another fightstick build. The left is the final build. The right are the internals. Cable management to be desired.

How long does it take for old to be cool? I was asked to save this Mad Catz Street Fighter IV Round 2 fightstick and get it compatible for the newest generation of games. We replaced the internal motherboard with a Brooks Universal Fighting Board for use in modern consoles. We cut and moved around internal supports to fit the new board. A hole was then cut out to fit a new back button.

Sound Voltex Modding

My Sound Voltex. The left is the final build. The right are the internals.

Sound Voltex is a popular Japanese arcade rhythm game featuring 6 buttons and 2 dials. My personal controller is a Gamo FauceTwo. The default parts were swapped out with Omron D2MV-01-1C3 switches with Sanwa OBSA-60UK and OBSA-45UK buttons. The dials were then slightly modified to be tighter by stuffing the internal dead zone and tuning the code disk.

Steam Deck Modding

My Steam Deck.

I’ve probably spent more time opening this thing open over playing with it. My Steam Deck is essentially fully custom, with a full shell swap (complete disassembly), SSD upgrade, and GuliKit Electromagnetic Joysticks. I’ve been experimenting with optimizing battery life and thermals on the hardware and software side, replacing thermal paste, liquid metal, and thermal pad channels. My deck is also undervolted and overclocked to maximize performance.
